Semantic interoperability (again)

  • Data structuring highly depends on cultural traditions:
    • My DTD is right, your is wrong
    • This is not an ENTITY, is an ATTRIBUTE of ENTITY xxx
  • Even a simple data item can be obscure:
    • Does everyone know what the "9 Thermidore" is?
    • Are we in the year 5765?
    • Maybe in the year 1382?
    • Quoted from a diary: "On 9/11/2001 I was in NYC. It was a pleasant day, and everything was going as planned."
  • The more sophisticated is the document structure, the less probable is the compatibility.
  • Even widely accepted standards, like Dublin Core or Bib-1 are sometimes used in a non consistent manner.
